## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

Let me warn you about failure modes I've seen in consulting businesses.

**Pitfall 1: Underpricing to Win First Clients**

It's tempting to offer discounts to get momentum. Resist this. Charge full rate from Day 1. Reasoning:

- Underpriced clients are often the most demanding (they're price-sensitive, not value-sensitive)
- You set a reference point—hard to raise rates later
- You devalue your positioning (if you're cheap, you must not be that good)

If a prospect can't afford your rate, phase the project smaller, don't discount your hourly-equivalent rate.

**Pitfall 2: Scope Creep**

Client asks for "just one more thing." You say yes to be helpful. Suddenly the $10K project became $25K of work.

Solution: Extremely detailed Statements of Work (SOW). Literally list every deliverable and explicitly state what's NOT included. When client requests additions, immediately send change order with additional cost.

**Pitfall 3: Overcommitting**

You close 5 projects in one month. Great! Except now you're working 80-hour weeks and burning out.

Solution: Limit concurrent projects to 4-5 max. Stagger start dates. Be willing to say "I'm at capacity until [date], but I can start your project then." Good clients will wait. Rushed work damages reputation.

**Pitfall 4: Not Collecting Payment**

Client loves the work. Asks for revisions. Promises payment next week. Next week becomes next month.

Solution: 50% deposit before starting. Remaining 50% before final delivery. No exceptions. If client can't pay, they can't afford you. Better to learn this before you invest 100 hours.

**Pitfall 5: Imposter Syndrome**

You'll sometimes think "Why would anyone pay me $50K for this?" Especially early on.

Counter-narrative: Your portfolio shows $867M in value creation. You've designed brain-computer interfaces, HIV treatment frameworks, and $215M acquisition strategies. You have mathematical rigor others don't. You can translate across technical and business domains. You're not an imposter—you're exceptionally qualified.
